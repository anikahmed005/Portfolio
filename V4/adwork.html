AdWork

About

AdWork is a conceptual application made to guide budding creatives whilst preparing them for the industry with professional projects.

The Problem

The Problem that I had sought to solve was something that I was facing myself. I had recently discovered & fell in love with UX design but had no guidance as to what I should do to pursue it as a career. Thus I decided that the problem should become: "How to help budding creatives build skills, habits, networks & a portfolio to guide them along in their respective career paths"

Research Methods: Where to Begin

Before I began to even think about any sort of design, I had to understand the people whom I was building this application for .
This required me to utilize a wide variety of research methodology ranging from online surveys, and in-person interviews.

Surveys

The Online Surveys were conducted via google forms. The questions that I had curated dealt with the emotional feeling of being lost, unfufilled, and generally unsure about one's creative process. whether it's building a portfolio or just finding that one job.
Aside from those kind of questions, I also focused upon the features that Adwork might consider having and asking the participants how they feel about specific features to gauge interest.
The survey was then distributed to student's from a wide variety of disciplines that may utilize this app such as communications technology, fashion design, graphic design & architecture.

Interviews

Interviews were conducted via in-person sitdowns or phone calls. The partcipants were selected from the small pool of 22 that had taken the survey, from that they were given a option to opt in for a interview. Only 7 of the 22 participants chose to opt in
The 7 interviewees were given a selection of questions in order to gauge their interest in features that would be included but also asked how they felt about specific creative tendencies related to these features.

Design Process

After seeing the results of my research methods, I began to start the design process for the project. For this specific project I decided start off step by step starting with;

  • Moodboard:

    Creating a moodboard revolves around finding inspiration from other applications in terms of layout/features, the typefaces and color schemes that I utilized.This provided me with insight that I would utilize when coming up with the paper prototype and the eventual digital prototype.

  • User Flow Chart:

    Creating a user flow chart is something that is integral to do before I sketched out my design ideas as it's supposed to to give me a 'pathway' to adhere to, that would make sense for the users that will use it and shape the 'outline' of my ideas as a whole. It allowed me to refocus my design so that it would make sense to users, and not look like a jumbled mess.

  • Sketches/Paper Prototype:

    After doing both the moodboard and flow chart, I began to draw out my design ideas in order to grasp the direction that I intend to follow. I did about 3 iterations of my sketches before I choose which one would be used as a paper prototype in order for me to begin a final digial prototype.

    • Final Thoughts, Stakeholders + Prototype

    Using all of my research and the foundation that I had set for the project with the data that I had collected from the in-person interviews, online surveys, alongside the design decisions I made based upon the paper prototype, moodboard, & flow chart, I've made a strong product for both students and mentors. That being said I do believe that there should be more iterations and testing regarding the product itself that need to be expanded upon.


    Furthermore to explain to supposed stakeholders (those who may be invested in the project outside of my primary targeted demographic i.e students/experienced veterns) as to why AdWork would be beenficial to fund, invest time towards, and support I'd state that AdWork would be beneficial in educating a group of people as a community, allow hiring departments of agencies use the app to scout/educate local talent, build a relationship with budding creatives as a way to develop your brand. This goes without to say that the monetary benefits of using the app to request work to be done for a nominal fee as opposed to freelancer rates.

    All in all AdWork is a great application in concept and I've thorooughly enjoyed the bulk of the work as it has introduced me to the world of user experience design as a whole. Thank you for your attention to my first case study.
